<p><p>Wideband couplers are a type of device used in radio frequency systems to divide and combine signals. The market for wideband couplers consists of two main types: wideband directional couplers and wideband hybrid couplers. </p><p>Wideband directional couplers are used to split a signal into two different paths. They allow a portion of the signal to be directed towards an output port while the remaining portion is passed through to another port.</p><p>On the other hand, wideband hybrid couplers are used to combine two signals while maintaining isolation between them. They split the incoming signals into two equal parts, with one part being combined and sent to an output port, while the other part is directed to another port.</p><p>Both types of wideband couplers are essential in various applications, such as telecommunications, wireless communication systems, radar systems, and test and measurement equipment.</p></p>
