<p><p>The anhydrous dibasic calcium phosphate market can be classified into two types based on the acid used for its production: hydrochloric acid and phosphoric acid market. In the hydrochloric acid market, anhydrous dibasic calcium phosphate is produced by reacting the acid with calcium carbonate. On the other hand, in the phosphoric acid market, the acid is reacted with calcium hydroxide to produce anhydrous dibasic calcium phosphate. These two types of market refer to the different methods of production employed in the manufacturing of anhydrous dibasic calcium phosphate.</p></p>
